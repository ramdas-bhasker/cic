The Southern Regional Committee of All India Council for Technical Education in their meeting held on 10-9-1970 decided to recommend to Government of India to start an independent institute of Commercial Practice at Hyderabad to serve the growing needs of the area, at the instance of Sri T.R. Dass, the then Director of Technical Education, A.P. The Committee felt that as, Hyderabad is the capital city of the then undivided state of Andhra Pradesh such a separate Institute here will be in a position in course of time, to be not only a model commerce institute in the professional fields for all such institutes of the State but also will be able to offer advanced and short-term professional courses in Commerce and Short Hand and Typing. Necessary faculty facilities are also available in twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad. Hence, the Committee recommended to start an independent Institution of Commerce with an initial intake of 60 per year, if the necessary physical facilities are made available to the Institute.

Established in 1972, the CENTRAL INSTITUTE OF COMMERCE was incepted in the year 1972 and sponsored by Exhibition Society.

Central Institute of Commerce is established as an Aided Polytechnic College benefiting the students especially of Telangana, in the course of DCCP- Diploma in Commercial and computer Practice.

Since inception, many students benefited from the course offered.

Most of the students are employed in various Central, State, Judicial Departments and Private Organizations in esteemed positions.

The faculty here is very experienced, and the students are very disciplined.

The State Government in 21-8-1972 have sanctioned an independent Institution of Commerce under the management of, Exhibition Society
